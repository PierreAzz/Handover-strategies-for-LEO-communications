Performance Comparison of LEO Satellite Handover Algorithms by Pierre EL KAZZI

Abstract

This paper investigates handover strategies for Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite communications, focusing on maximizing throughput while minimizing handover frequency and signaling overhead. Three algorithms are compared: classical HM+FH+MWM, HM+FH+MWM with Time-to-Trigger (TTT), and a novel multi-metric LIHA combining link gain, throughput, and stability with TTT. Performance is evaluated via simulation over multiple gateways and time slots. Results demonstrate that incorporating TTT and multi-metric scoring significantly reduces handovers while maintaining high throughput.

System Model

A LEO satellite network with multiple gateways and satellites is considered. At each time slot, link gain G(g,s,t) and throughput R(g,s,t) are measured. Handover cost is modeled as one unit of energy per handover.

Handover Algorithms

HM+FH+MWM (Classical)

Greedy algorithm that assigns each gateway to the satellite with the highest instantaneous link gain. Frequent handovers are possible due to rapid fluctuations.

HM+FH+MWM + TTT

Enhances classical HM+FH+MWM by introducing a Hysteresis Margin (HM) and Time-to-Trigger (TTT) to prevent ping-pong handovers. Only switches if the new satellite outperforms current by HM for TTT consecutive slots.

LIHA (Multi-Metric + TTT)

The proposed Low Interference Handover Algorithm (LIHA) uses a multi-metric score:

score(s) = α G(g,s,t) + β R(g,s,t)/R_max + γ S(g,s,t)

Where α, β, γ are weights, R_max is maximum throughput, and S(g,s,t) is a stability factor. HM + TTT logic is applied to this score to decide handovers.
